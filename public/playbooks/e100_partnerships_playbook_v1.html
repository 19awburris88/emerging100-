What Makes Money Move
White glove service. Partners stay because they feel valued, seen, and connected to impact. The relationship doesn't end when the check clears — it deepens. Regular touchpoints, impact data, and genuine care for their KPIs is what keeps sponsors coming back year after year.
You must have GRIT. Fundraising is a long game. Rejections happen. Timelines slip. The ask requires resilience, follow-through, and the willingness to hear no five times before getting a yes. The Partnerships team must be the most persistent people in the org.
You must be a storyteller. The kids and the work tell the story. A Partnerships person who can walk a corporate executive from a BEST Academy session to a C100 Career Fair to a Gala scholarship reveal — that's the pitch. Classroom → College Campus → Corporation. Connect those dots and the money follows.

The Ask Pipeline — New Partner Process
The ask begins in the fall. But the relationship that makes the ask possible is built year-round. Partnerships doesn't cold call — they build warm relationships first, understand what the partner cares about, then make a tailored ask that feels like a natural next step.
01
Identify the Warm Lead
New partners come from three places: (1) companies that have served with E100 through community service — flagged by the CS Chair, (2) existing relationships held by the President or Exec Board, (3) companies identified through research that align with E100's mission. Partnerships Chair maintains the prospect list in Monday.com.
Owner: Partnerships Chair + Community Service Chair (on-ramp leads)
Tool: Monday.com prospect pipeline
02
Research Their Goals First
Before any outreach, understand what the company cares about. What are their CSR priorities? DEI commitments? Community investment targets? Which of their KPIs can E100 help them hit? The pitch is not "here's what we do" — it's "here's how we help you do what you care about."
Owner: Partnerships Chair / Account Manager
Tool: Company website, LinkedIn, annual reports, CSR disclosures
03
Warm Introduction
Where possible, the President or a mutual contact makes the intro. This is where Exec Board relationships are activated — they open the door, then hand the relationship to the Partnerships team. A warm intro is worth 10 cold emails. The Partner Account Manager system being built out formalizes this handoff.
Owner: President / Exec Board → hands off to Partnerships Chair
Tool: Email intro + Monday.com account log
04
Virtual Discovery Meeting
First meeting is virtual. No ask yet. The goal is to listen — learn their goals, their challenges, what they want from a community partnership. Share E100's story. Walk them through the classroom → college → corporation pipeline. Let the data and the mission do the work. This is where you earn the right to come back.
Owner: Partnerships Chair + Exec Board presence if relationship-appropriate
Tool: Sponsorship deck (impact data + program overview) · Zoom/Teams
05
Tailor the Proposal
Based on the discovery meeting, build a custom proposal that aligns E100's programs with their specific goals. Don't send a generic deck — show them exactly where they fit. Which program? Which event? Which students would their dollars reach? The more specific, the more compelling. Include impact data, program outcomes, and past partner logos for social proof.
Owner: Partnerships Chair
Tool: Customized sponsorship deck (Fiverr contact for design refresh)
06
Make the Ask
Present the proposal in a follow-up meeting — in person if possible. Name the tier. Name the dollar amount. Name what they get. Be direct. The ask season is fall — but relationship conversations happen year-round so that by the time fall arrives, the ask is not a cold conversation. It's a formality.
Owner: Partnerships Chair + President (for anchor-level asks)
Tool: Proposal deck + sponsorship agreement
07
Close & Onboard
Agreement signed. Payment received. Onboard the partner — introduce them to the programs they're sponsoring, give them the year's event calendar, set expectations for how they'll be recognized. This is where white glove service begins. Their first experience as an official E100 partner sets the tone for the entire relationship.
Owner: Partnerships Chair
Tool: Sponsorship agreement · Monday.com account update · Welcome email
08
Steward Year-Round
The relationship doesn't end when the check clears. Quarterly check-ins minimum. Send impact updates after every program event they funded. Tag them in social content. Invite them to events. Show them the students. Keep them in the E100 family so that when renewal season comes, saying yes is the easiest decision they make all year.
Owner: Partnerships Chair / Account Manager
Tool: Monday.com touchpoint log · Impact reports · Social media tags
Renewal Strategy
⚑ Renewals need an overhaul. Currently organic — relationships drive re-ups but there's no formal timeline or process. This section documents the recommended renewal strategy. Needs to be reviewed and adopted by the Partnerships Chair.
Recommended Renewal Timeline
6mo
6 Months Before Renewal — Mid-Year Impact Update
Send every active partner a mid-year impact update. Not an ask — a report. Show them what their dollars have done so far. Program data, photos, student quotes. Remind them they're making a difference. This is pure stewardship. It also seeds the renewal conversation so it doesn't come out of nowhere.
Owner: Partnerships Chair + Data Dept (impact data)
3mo
3 Months Before — Renewal Conversation
Schedule a check-in call or meeting with every partner coming up for renewal. Ask how the partnership has been going. Ask what they want more of. Ask what could be better. This is not the ask yet — it's the conversation that sets up the ask. Learn if their priorities have shifted. See if there's an opportunity to upgrade them.
Owner: Partnerships Chair
Tool: Monday.com renewal pipeline — flag all partners 3 months out
6wk
6 Weeks Before — Send Renewal Proposal
Send the formal renewal proposal with the new year's opportunities, updated impact data from the current year, and a specific ask. If there's an opportunity to upgrade their tier, include that option. Give them a clear deadline — 4 weeks to respond. Early commitments get early recognition.
Owner: Partnerships Chair
Tool: Updated sponsorship deck + renewal agreement
✓
Close Renewals Before Fall Ask Season Begins
The goal is to have all returning partners renewed before the fall ask season starts. This means the fall is spent on new business — not re-closing people who should already be in. Renewals closed early = more capacity for growth. Any partner not renewed by October needs a personal call from the President.
Owner: Partnerships Chair — escalate to President if stalled

The Handoff Process (Recommended)
⚑ Currently blurry — needs to be formalized. The Exec Board holds many relationships. The Gala team does some outreach independently. Partnerships does some outreach. There's no clear lane. This recommended process defines the lanes.
01
Partnerships Owns the Relationship Year-Round
From January through the Gala, Partnerships is the primary point of contact for every corporate sponsor. Gala team does not independently reach out to sponsors — all sponsor communication flows through Partnerships or is coordinated with them.
02
Partnerships Closes Sponsorships — Briefs Gala Team
Once a sponsorship is closed, Partnerships briefs the Gala team: who the sponsor is, what tier, what they get (tables, logo placement, recognition), and who their contact is. Gala team takes it from there for event logistics and activation.
03
Shared Sponsor List — Real Time
Both teams need access to a live sponsor list in Monday.com showing who's confirmed, what they've paid, what they're owed, and their contact. No version control issues, no one working off stale spreadsheets. One source of truth.
Tool: Monday.com — shared Gala sponsor board
04
Gala Activates — Partnerships Stewards After
On the night of the Gala, Gala team owns the experience. After the Gala, Partnerships owns the follow-up — thank you notes, impact wrap-up, and beginning the renewal conversation. The Gala is not the end of the relationship. It's the highlight of it.

Every question a brand new Partnerships Chair would need answered before they could plan.
🔴 Blocker
Who are our current active partners and what is their commitment status? Which companies have signed for this year, which are in negotiation, and which are up for renewal? Without this I can't make a single outreach call.
🔴 Blocker
Who do I NOT contact because the President or Exec Board owns that relationship? Some relationships (Blank Foundation, Delta in-kind) are held at President level. If I call the wrong person I could damage the relationship.
🔴 Blocker
Where is the current sponsorship deck and is it approved? I can't send anything to a prospect without the current, approved deck. Where does it live and is it up to date?
🔴 Blocker
What partners are up for renewal right now? The renewal timeline is 6mo → 3mo → 6wk. Which companies are in each phase and what is the next step for each?
🔴 Blocker
What is in Monday.com and what is not? The CRM is the source of truth — but how complete is it? Who updates it and am I responsible for keeping it current?
📋 Board
What is the revenue goal for this year? Is there a specific fundraising target I'm accountable to? Who do I report progress against that target and how often?
📋 Board
What grants are open or upcoming right now? The grant pipeline is supposed to be proactive — but where is the current list of opportunities and past applications?
📋 Board
Who has Community Service flagged as a warm lead recently? CS is the best partner on-ramp — but only if intel gets to me. What is the current handoff process?
📋 Board
Partner Appreciation is in July — how much planning time do I have? Who owns the event logistics vs. the invite list vs. the impact presentation?
ℹ️ Info
What are the formal sponsorship tiers and pricing? I know the Gala tiers — but what is the full-year pricing for non-Gala sponsors? Is there a tiered benefits structure?
ℹ️ Info
Who is the Fiverr design contact for the sponsorship deck? Name, rate, turnaround time, and how do I brief them on updates?
ℹ️ Info
Which must-hit companies do I target every year without exception? Target, Blank Foundation, Gas South, Delta, Bank of America — what is the status of each and who owns each relationship?
