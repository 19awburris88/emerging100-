The Structural Reality
Comms is a service department. It does not create programs — it amplifies them. The quality of what Comms can post is entirely dependent on the quality of what Chairs and committees submit. A Comms team cannot tell a story without content. Every Chair in the org has a responsibility to feed this machine.
The current gap: Chairs are inconsistent in submitting content and captions. Approval from Exec Board sometimes creates delays. The result: moments that should be celebrated publicly go unposted, and the org's story goes untold. This playbook defines the process that closes that gap on both sides.
Exec Board oversight is intentional. More than any other department, what E100 posts publicly represents the org's brand, values, and relationships. President and VP must approve before anything goes live. This is the right call — but the approval process must be fast enough not to kill timeliness.
The North Star — E100's Story
"The Emerging 100 of Atlanta serves as role models in the community, helping grow the next leadership ecosystem."
E100 Brand Voice — Every Caption Should Feel This
What the Story Sounds Like
Comms must flow as a story. Each post is a chapter. Each chapter connects to the next. The audience should be able to scroll the E100 feed and feel the arc — from a BEST Academy session, to a C100 career fair, to a Gala scholarship reveal, to a community service event. It's one continuous story of investment, mentorship, and impact.

The students are always the hero of the story. BEST Academy students, C100 scholars, scholarship recipients. Their faces, their stories, their futures. Always lead with the youth when they're in the content.
🤝

The relationships between E100 members and the students they mentor. These are the moments that show what E100 actually does — not just what it says it does.
💼
The Work
The sessions, the events, the service. Show E100 in action — not just at galas and social events, but in a school library on a Friday afternoon doing the work.
🏢
The Sponsors + Partners
Celebrate the companies that make it possible. Tag them, feature them, show their investment in action. This is both gratitude and stewardship — partners see themselves in the story.
💬

Pull quotes from students, scholars, mentors. Tug on heart strings. The Rites of Passage tie-passing, the scholarship check moment, the student speaking about their future — these are the posts that move people.

Content Workflow — From Event to Post
This is the full lifecycle of a piece of content. Every post starts with an event or program moment and ends with a published post on Instagram or LinkedIn. The Comms team manages steps 4–7. Everything before step 4 is the responsibility of the department Chair.
01
Event Happens
A BEST session, C100 event, community service day, Gala, social fundraiser — any program or event across any department. The moment happens.
Owner: Department Chair
02
Photo + Video Captured
Donald Gibson (primary POC) or other assigned photographers/videographers capture the event. This is booked in advance by the Operations chair of the relevant department. 2 photographers and 2–3 videographers for major events (Gala, Social Fundraisers). 1–2 for program sessions.
Owner: Department Operations Chair + Donald Gibson
⚑ Turnaround: Photographers return content within ~1 week. This is the first variable that affects posting timelines.
03
Chair Submits Content Package to Comms
The department Chair submits the full content package to Comms within 48 hours of receiving content back from the photographer. Package includes: selected photos (curated, not the full album), video clip(s), draft caption, any sponsor/partner tags to include, and context notes. Use the standard submission format (see Submission Standard tab).
Owner: Department Chair
⚑ This is the current gap. Chairs are inconsistent. The submission standard in this playbook fixes that.
04
Comms Receives + Organizes
Comms Chair receives the package. Reviews the content — combs through the album carefully. Checks all photos against brand and sensitivity guidelines (member with unknown partner, inappropriate backgrounds, etc.). Selects the best 1–3 images or video for the post. Refines the caption if needed while preserving the chair's voice and context.
Owner: Comms Chair
05
Draft Prepared + Sent for Approval
Comms Chair prepares the final draft post — image(s) selected, caption finalized, tags and hashtags added, platform format confirmed (Instagram vs. LinkedIn may differ). Draft sent to President and VP (or designated brand reviewer) for approval before scheduling.
Owner: Comms Chair → President + VP
⚑ Approval must happen within 24 hours of submission to maintain posting timeline. If no response in 24hrs, Comms Chair escalates.
06
Exec Board Approves
President and VP (or designated approver) review and approve. They may request caption edits, photo swaps, or tag adjustments. Comms makes revisions and resubmits if needed. Once approved, the post is scheduled.
Owner: President + VP
07
Post Scheduled + Published
Comms schedules the post according to the posting calendar — max twice a day, once on Fridays, light on weekends. Posts go live on Instagram and/or LinkedIn depending on content type. Content calendar updated.
Owner: Comms Chair
08
Engagement Monitored + Data Collected
After posting, Comms monitors engagement — especially for Social Fundraiser content where campaign data (interactions, likes, reshares, reach) is needed for sponsor ROI reports. Data pulled and delivered to the relevant department Chair and Partnerships team after each major event.
Owner: Comms Chair + Department Chair

Approval Process
Nothing posts without Exec Board approval. This is the highest-oversight department in E100 for good reason. The President and VP (or designated brand reviewer) must sign off on every post before it goes live. The process must be fast enough to stay timely.
01
Comms Prepares Draft
Comms Chair takes the submission from the department Chair, selects the best image(s), refines the caption, adds tags and hashtags, and formats for both Instagram and LinkedIn. Draft is complete and ready for review.
Owner: Comms Chair
02
Draft Sent to President + VP
Comms Chair sends the draft via an agreed channel (text, email, or a dedicated approval thread — to be defined). Include: the image(s), the caption, the tags, which platform(s), and the proposed posting day/time. Make it easy to say yes — give them everything in one message.
Owner: Comms Chair → President + VP
Tool: Text (current) · A dedicated approval tool is recommended
03
24-Hour Response Window
President or VP must respond within 24 hours — approve, request edits, or escalate. If no response in 24 hours, Comms Chair sends one follow-up. If still no response after 12 more hours, escalates to the other Exec Board member. Time-sensitive posts (live events, breaking moments) get a shorter window — flag these explicitly.
Owner: President + VP
⚑ The 24-hour window is the standard. Breaking it consistently kills timeliness and demoralizes the Comms team.
04
Approved → Schedule. Edits Requested → Revise + Resubmit.
If approved: Comms schedules the post immediately. If edits are requested: Comms makes the changes and resubmits for a final check. The revision cycle should not exceed one round — if there's a fundamental disagreement about direction, that's a conversation, not an edit.
Owner: Comms Chair
What Requires Approval vs. What Doesn't
Always Requires Approval
  • All regular program and event posts
  • Any post featuring students or youth
  • Sponsor and partner tags/mentions
  • Collab posts with other accounts
  • Anything related to political topics or civic events
  • Posts mentioning specific members by name
  • Any post with a quote attributed to someone
  • Crisis or sensitive topic responses
May Move Faster (Still Needs Approval)
  • Live event coverage — flag as time-sensitive, get verbal approval
  • Reposting content E100 is tagged in — quick check still needed
  • National awareness day posts using pre-approved templates
  • Member milestone posts (birthdays, promotions) — Chair pre-approves format
Collaboration Post Policy
E100 is wary of who it collab posts with. A collab post puts another account's audience directly in E100's feed — and vice versa. Before any collab post, the Comms Chair must vet the account: who are they, what do they post, is there any content that conflicts with E100's values or brand? President approves all collab posts. No exceptions.

Photo + Album Review Rules
Every album must be combed before anything is posted. After Social Fundraisers especially — 1,200+ attendees means photos of people E100 doesn't know, in contexts that weren't planned. Review every photo before selecting. These are the specific things to look for:
Flag Before Posting
  • Any E100 member pictured with a woman — if marital status or relationship is unknown, do not post without checking first
  • Photos of minors — confirm parental consent before posting any student under 18
  • Background content — what's visible behind the subject? Anything inappropriate?
  • Unintended people in frame — attendees who didn't consent to being featured
  • Any photo that could be taken out of context
  • Alcohol prominently featured — especially near youth content
What Makes a Great Post Photo
  • Youth or students in authentic moments — not posed
  • E100 members mentoring, teaching, serving — showing the work
  • Scholarship check presentation moments — high emotion
  • Group energy at events — shows community and scale
  • Corporate partners engaging with students or members
  • Behind-the-scenes prep — shows effort and commitment

The E100 Caption Formula & Voice Guide
The voice of Emerging 100 of Atlanta. Every caption, every post, every platform. This is not a style suggestion — it is the standard.
The E100 Voice in One Sentence
"Professional yet personable, elevated yet authentic, and always rooted in purpose, community impact, and the belief that leadership is measured by service."
The 6 Voice Pillars
01 · Purpose-Driven, Not Promotional
Every caption answers: why does this moment matter?
We never post just to post. Every caption earns its place by connecting to mission, impact, or community. This is the heart of the E100 voice.
02 · Warm, Human, and Real
Avoid corporate tone. Avoid AI-sounding phrasing.
Human-centered framing in everything. If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it. If it could have been written by a bot, rewrite it.
03 · Confident but Never Boastful
Humble impact tone — strong, aspirational, grounded.
We are proud of what we do without needing to announce it loudly. Let the work speak. Let the people speak. Frame it with confidence, not ego.
04 · Community-First Orientation
Always center "we," "our mentees," "our partners," "our city."
This isn't about E100 as an organization — it's about what E100 does for people. Lead with the community, not the committee.
05 · Action-Oriented Language
Captions always invite — participation, support, mentorship, engagement.
Every post has a forward lean. Even retrospective posts point toward what's next. We close with momentum, gratitude, or invitation — never a period followed by silence.
06 · Premium but Approachable
Elevated, intentional, but never stiff.
We are a professional organization that shows up in communities, schools, and boardrooms. The voice reflects all of that — it can move between warmth and gravitas without losing either.
Caption Structure
1
Strong Hook
Sets intention. Stops the scroll. Answers "why should I keep reading?" in the first line. Not a description of the event — a reason to care.
2
Story or Impact
Centers people, not logistics. What happened? Who was there? What did it mean? Lead with the human moment, not the event name or date.
3
Mission Connection
Mentorship, leadership, community. Connect the moment back to why E100 exists. Even a social event has a mission hook — use it.
4
Call to Action or Reflection
Momentum, gratitude, or invitation. Close with forward energy. Tag partners. Acknowledge people. Point toward what's next. Never end flat.

⚑ Open Items — To Finalize
These are the structural gaps in how Comms currently operates. Most of these are process decisions that need to be made by the Comms Chairs and Exec Board together.
01
🔴 Approval Channel — Formalize It — Currently using text. A dedicated tool (WhatsApp group, Slack, or Monday.com) is recommended to create a cleaner approval trail. The channel needs to be formalized, agreed upon, and used consistently. Ambiguity here is what kills the 24-hour turnaround.
Owner: Comms Chair + President + VP
02
🔴 Caption Submission Standard — Enforce It — The submission format in this playbook needs to be distributed to every Chair and treated as mandatory. A Chair who submits content without a caption draft or context notes gets their content returned, not posted. Comms Chair must hold this line.
Owner: Comms Chair + Exec Board (enforce with Chairs)
03
🔴 Content Calendar Tool — Comms needs a content calendar to plan posts in advance, track what's scheduled, and prevent gaps. Options: Later, Buffer, Meta Business Suite (free), or a simple Monday.com board. Define, adopt, and document the tool.
Owner: Comms Chair
04
Photographer/Videographer Delivery SLA — All photographers and videographers need a formal delivery timeline documented — not just Donald Gibson. Every media vendor should have a defined turnaround standard so Comms can plan posting. — how many days after an event do they return content? This timeline feeds everything downstream. If it's not defined, Comms can't plan.
Owner: Comms Chair + Donald Gibson
05
Member Photo Sensitivity Policy — Formalize — The policy around not posting members with women of unknown relationship status needs to be formally documented and communicated to all Chairs and photographers. Currently this lives only as institutional knowledge.
Owner: President + Comms Chair
06
Student / Minor Consent Process — BEST Academy students are minors. Before any student under 18 is featured on E100 social media, there must be a documented consent process. Work with BEST Academy administration to define and formalize this.
Owner: Comms Chair + BEST Chair + Ray Singer (BEST Admin)
07
AI Caption Generation — Explore — You mentioned potentially exploring AI to generate captions. This is worth piloting. AI can generate first drafts from submission notes, dramatically reducing the load on both Chairs and Comms. Define the workflow and test it on a low-stakes post first.
Owner: Comms Chair
08
3 Comms Chairs — Role Definition — There are 3 Chairs + Advisors. Who owns what? Does one own Instagram and one own LinkedIn? Does one own event content and one own graphic assets? Define lanes so no one is doing everything and nothing falls through.
Owner: Comms Chairs + Exec Board
09
Campaign Data Delivery Process — After each Social Fundraiser, who pulls the data, in what format, and delivers it to whom? This process needs to be standardized so every sponsor gets a consistent ROI report every time.
Owner: Comms Chair + Social Fundraising Chair + Partnerships Chair

No rigid swim lane split. Even sharing across caption writing, Instagram, Threads, and LinkedIn. All three Chairs own all three platforms collectively — workload is distributed, not siloed.
