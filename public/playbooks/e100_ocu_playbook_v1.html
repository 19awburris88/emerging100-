OCU's North Star: Civic responsibility is part of E100's identity. The same energy E100 brings to mentoring students, it brings to showing up as active citizens in Atlanta. OCU ensures E100 is never silent on civic participation — while staying firmly non-partisan.
Voting engagement is flexible by design. OCU will not always run a Votelanta-style event. The activities depend entirely on what elections are happening in Atlanta — mayoral, city council, school board, state, federal. OCU monitors the election calendar and activates accordingly. When there's nothing to vote on, OCU focuses on clean-up, civic education, and community discourse.
OCU Events
OCU-01 · Election-Dependent
Voting Engagement Events
Non-partisan voting engagement activities tied to local Atlanta elections — mayoral, city council, school board, state, or federal. Format varies: voter registration drives, candidate forums, polling location information sessions, get-out-the-vote activations. Votelanta (October 2025, The Eastern) is an example — not a permanent annual event. OCU monitors the Atlanta election calendar and designs the appropriate activation. When there's a major election, OCU mobilizes. When there isn't, OCU pivots to civic education.
Non-Partisan · Election-Dependent
OCU-02 · Annual · December
Community Street Clean-Up
Annual community clean-up event in December. 2025: Almond Park & Carey Park. OCU owns this fully. E100 members show up for the city in a tangible, visible way. Aligns with International Volunteer Day (December 5). Simple, impactful, and highly visible — strong social media and community credibility moment. Coordinate with Comms for content capture.
Annual · December
OCU-03 · As Needed
Civic Education + Discourse Events
Forums, town halls, and community conversations on issues affecting Atlanta's Black community. Policy briefings, candidate education (non-partisan), census participation, economic empowerment. These activate when the moment calls for them — not on a fixed annual schedule. OCU Chair monitors the civic calendar and proposes events to Exec Board accordingly.
As Needed · Civic Moment Driven
Voting Engagement Framework
OCU does not endorse candidates. Ever. The role is to inform, register, and mobilize — not to tell the community who to vote for. This non-partisan stance protects E100's relationships with corporate partners, city leaders, and community members across the political spectrum.
