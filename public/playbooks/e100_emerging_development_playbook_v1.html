Why This Committee Matters
You can't pour from an empty cup. E100 members give a tremendous amount to students, to the community, to partners. Emerging Development makes sure someone is giving back to the members. Brotherhood is what keeps people here. If members feel like E100 is just another obligation, they go inactive. If they feel like E100 is their people — they stay forever.
This committee has the most untapped potential in the org. The events that have already happened — New Member Meetup, 15th Anniversary, Men's Mental Health, the upcoming BBQ — are proof of concept. The opportunity to build leadership circles, connect C100 into the membership pipeline, and create moments that invest in members' professional and personal growth is enormous.
North Star — What ED Is Building
Every member should feel seen, valued, and invested in. Not just as a volunteer. Not just as a chair. As a person. Emerging Development creates the moments that remind members why they joined and why they stay.
The Three Pillars of ED
🤝
Brotherhood
The relationships between members. The trust, the camaraderie, the sense of belonging to something bigger than yourself. Brotherhood is built in the informal moments — not just the formal ones.
📈
Investment
Investing in members' professional and personal growth. Leadership development, wellness, skills. E100 should make every member a better professional, a better leader, and a better person.
🔗
Pipeline
C100 young men who age into the professional world are E100's most natural future members. ED is the bridge. Engage them now so the transition feels like a continuation, not a cold start.
What "Member Engagement" Actually Means
Retention
Keep Members Active
An active member is a dues-paying member. A member who feels connected doesn't go inactive. ED's events are what keep the org from losing good people to drift.
Belonging
Everyone Feels at Home
New members especially. The New Member Meetup exists so no one joins E100 and feels like a stranger for their first year.
Recognition
Members Feel Seen
Milestones, promotions, life events — ED finds ways to acknowledge members as people, not just volunteers.
Growth
Better People, Better Leaders
Leadership circles, professional development, mental health — invest in the whole member, not just their committee role.
Known Events + History

ED-01 · Annual · Recurring
New Member Meetup
Staple Event

The official welcome for the incoming class. All existing E100 members meet the new class. The moment that turns strangers into brothers. Critical for new member retention — a member who doesn't feel connected in year one rarely stays for year two.

Purpose
  • All members meet the incoming class face-to-face
  • New members feel welcomed and seen immediately
  • Sets the tone for brotherhood from day one
  • Organic relationship building — not forced networking
  • Removes the "stranger in the room" feeling for new members
Planning Notes
  • Timed with incoming class arrival. Past location: Tessarae Hotel (location can change). Always right after a new class is inducted. — coordinate with Process & Governance on new member cycle
  • Casual format — social, food, conversation
  • Every existing member should attend — this is a membership expectation
  • Photo and recap to Comms within 48hrs
  • Budget: confirm with Treasurer
ED-02 · Annual · In Planning
Member BBQ
Annual Social

The annual member social — informal, celebratory, brotherhood-first. A moment where members can just be themselves outside of a committee role or a program responsibility. Food, music, good company. This is the culture event of the year for E100 members.

Purpose + Format
  • Pure member appreciation — no program agenda
  • Casual outdoor format — BBQ, music, games
  • Families welcome (TBD — confirm annually)
  • Builds the informal relationships that create real brotherhood
  • Can incorporate a light recognition moment for members
Planning Notes
  • Currently in planning — timing and venue TBD
  • Food and catering: confirm vendor and budget
  • C100 young men — consider inviting as a pipeline moment
  • Photo and recap to Comms
  • ⚑ Open item: lock date, venue, and budget

Celebration

Major org milestones celebrated as events. The 15th Anniversary was E100's most significant. These are planned as they occur — not every year, but when the org hits a moment worth marking. ED owns these when they happen.

ED-04 · Annual · Recurring
Men's Mental Health Event
Wellness

A dedicated space for E100 members to address mental health, stress, and emotional wellness. Intentional and vulnerable. The work E100 members do — mentoring, serving, leading — is taxing. This event acknowledges that and gives members permission to be human.

Purpose
  • Normalize mental health conversations among men
  • Give members a space to be vulnerable without judgment
  • Align with May Mental Health Awareness Month or other awareness moments
  • Professional facilitator or clinical partner recommended
  • Connects to Health & Wellness committee — coordinate
Planning Notes
  • Private/internal event — not public-facing
  • Clinical or professional facilitator engaged
  • No social media posting without member consent
  • Small group format works better than large
  • Potential to expand to C100 young men as well
Opportunity Space — What ED Could Own
This is the most wide-open committee in E100. The events already done are proof of concept. What follows is the full vision for what Emerging Development could build — some are near-term, some are longer-horizon, all are worth pursuing.
🏆
Leadership Circles
Small, intimate peer groups within E100 where members learn from each other. Think mastermind format — 6–8 members meeting regularly to share challenges, wins, and growth. Builds deep brotherhood and develops the next generation of E100 chairs and exec board members.
Future Initiative
🎓
C100 → E100 Pipeline
C100 young men who are graduating and entering the professional world are E100's most natural future members. ED creates intentional touchpoints — invite C100 seniors to a BBQ, to a New Member Meetup, to see what E100 looks like from the inside. Make the transition feel like a continuation.
High Priority — Build This
🌐
Atlanta Leadership Org Fair
Bring in other Atlanta leadership organizations — 100 Black Men, Urban League Young Professionals, similar groups — and let members and C100 sign up, learn, and connect. E100 members grow, and E100's reputation as a connector in the leadership ecosystem grows with it.
Explore
💼
Member Professional Development
Workshops, sessions, and speakers specifically for E100 members' career growth. Not for students — for the members themselves. Resume reviews, leadership skill sessions, financial planning, entrepreneurship. What you pour into members, they pour back into the mission.
Future Initiative
🧠
Mental Health Series
Expand the one-time Men's Mental Health event into a recurring series. Multiple formats — large group awareness, small group support circles, 1-on-1 resources. Partner with a clinical org (Kaiser Permanente, NAACP Health Equity) for facilitation. Normalize the conversation year-round.
Expand Existing
🎉
Member Recognition Program
Formal and informal ways to celebrate members beyond the Gala awards. Member of the month, birthday shoutouts, promotion announcements, service milestones. Small gestures of recognition build loyalty. ED owns this culture of celebration.
Explore
🤝
Alumni Engagement
Former E100 members are an untapped resource — for mentorship, for fundraising, for the C100 pipeline. ED creates the touchpoints that keep alumni connected to the org. Anniversary events, alumni panels at C100 events, a simple alumni communication channel.
Explore
🏌️
Member Social Series (including potential Brotherhood Thanksgiving)
Beyond the BBQ — a light calendar of member social moments throughout the year. Top Golf (already done for C100 mentorship), bowling, game nights, service trips. Informal touchpoints that don't feel like work. The best membership retention tool is members genuinely enjoying each other's company.

New Chair Questions
Every question a brand new Emerging Development Chair would need answered before they could plan.
🔴 Blocker
What events are confirmed for this year vs. TBD? New Member Meetup and Super Bowl Party are recurring — but what is the plan for Brotherhood BBQ and the November slot? I can't plan if the calendar is blank.
🔴 Blocker
How do I know which members are active and dues-paying? Active = dues-paying. Where is the list? Who maintains it? Without this I can't invite the right people to ED events.
🔴 Blocker
When exactly is the New Member Meetup relative to induction? It always happens right after the new class — but when does induction happen and who tells me the date so I can plan accordingly?
🔴 Blocker
What is my budget per event? Venue, food, entertainment — who approves the spend and what is the process to access funds?
📋 Board
Is the BBQ happening this year? Currently in planning — who makes the final call and when? I can't keep it in planning indefinitely.
📋 Board
What is the expectation for the Super Bowl Party? The direction is to lean into it more. What does that mean — bigger venue, more programming, corporate tie-in? What does success look like?
📋 Board
How does the C100-to-E100 pipeline work from my end? Do I plan a specific event to welcome graduating C100 seniors? Or does C100 Chair flag them and I follow up?
ℹ️ Info
What did last year's New Member Meetup look like? Tessarae Hotel was the venue — what was the format, how many attended, and what feedback did we get?
ℹ️ Info
