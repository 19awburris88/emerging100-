P&G is the org's institutional backbone. Programs come and go. Chairs change. Exec Boards turn over. Process & Governance ensures E100's standards, elections, brand, and agreements survive every transition. If it's a rule or a document that governs how E100 operates — P&G owns it.

Brand Guidelines
P&G owns E100's brand standards. How the org looks, how it presents itself, and how it maintains visual and tonal consistency across all materials — from the Comms team's Instagram posts to the Gala program booklet to sponsor decks. Brand standards protect E100's credibility.
Logo Usage

Which versions of the E100 logo can be used, on what backgrounds, at what sizes, and by whom. No unauthorized variations.

Rule Enforcement
P&G is the referee. Note: P&G (via Parliamentarian) currently runs background checks for BEST Academy mentors — this may expand to other contexts. When a member or committee violates E100's bylaws, standards of conduct, or meeting expectations — P&G is the body that addresses it. This is not punitive by nature. It is about maintaining the integrity of the org.

Contracts & Signing Authority
P&G assists with any contracts or documents the org needs executed. This is not legal counsel — it is organizational review. P&G ensures documents are appropriate before the President or authorized signatory signs on behalf of E100.

✓ Answered
Who are the authorized signatories?
Authorized signatories are the the President and the authorized organizational signatory. No contract or agreement is executed without one of these parties signing. P&G Chair ensures all agreements are routed through the correct signatory before anything is finalized.
📎 Work Through Later
Unresolved compliance issues from last year
No outstanding compliance issues at this time. If issues arise around attendance, conduct, or dues, P&G Chair works through them with the Parliamentarian. Document any issues here as they come up during the year.
✓ Answered
Does a formal conduct policy exist — who owns it?
Conduct policy is owned by the Parliamentarian. If a formal written policy does not exist or needs updating, P&G Chair works with the Parliamentarian to draft or revise it — not independently.
✓ Answered
Where are brand assets stored?
Brand assets — logo files, color palette, fonts, approved templates — are held by the Parliamentarian. Any committee needing brand assets goes to the Parliamentarian first, then to P&G Chair if needed. Do not circulate unapproved or outdated brand files.
