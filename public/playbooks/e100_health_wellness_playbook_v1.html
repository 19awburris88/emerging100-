The North Star
"We cannot fix anyone — but we can ensure they have access." This is the operating philosophy of Health & Wellness. E100 is not a clinical organization. It is a connector. When someone leaves a Health & Wellness event, they should leave with a resource, a contact, a next step — not just a feeling. Access is the product.
Community-facing, not internal. Unlike Emerging Development (which invests in members), Health & Wellness serves the broader Atlanta community. These are public events. Anyone can attend. This is E100 showing up for the city — not just the students it directly mentors.
Align to awareness moments. Men's Mental Health Month (June), Financial Literacy Month (April), National Minority Health Month (April), Black Mental Health Matters Week — the calendar gives H&W natural hooks for each event. Meet the community where the conversation is already happening.
3 Pillars — 3 Chairs
Each Chair owns their pillar end-to-end. They are responsible for the annual event in their pillar, the partners and speakers they bring in, and the resources they make available to attendees. The H&W Committee Chair coordinates all three and ensures they tell a coherent story across the year.
🧠
Mental Wellness
Chair 1 · Mental Wellness
Addressing mental health challenges, stress, emotional resilience, and psychological wellbeing. Uses accessible formats — including creative approaches like comedy — to lower the barrier of entry for difficult conversations. Laughing Matters is the signature event in this pillar.
💰
Financial Wellness
Chair 2 · Financial Wellness
Budgeting, saving, debt management, investing, homeownership, wealth-building. Financial wellness is health. Stress about money is one of the leading drivers of mental health challenges. Connecting these dots for the community is the work.
💪
Physical Wellness
Chair 3 · Physical Wellness
Physical fitness, preventive health, health screenings, nutrition. Particularly relevant in the context of health disparities facing Black men and the Black community broadly. Getting the community moving and getting them checked is the mission.
The 4th Element — Community Wellness
Beyond the three pillars, H&W veers into community wellness — events focused on housing access and equity-focused bootcamps. These aren't clinical wellness events — they're about removing systemic barriers that affect community health. Housing instability is a health issue. Economic inequity is a health issue. H&W can own this conversation.
Housing Access Events
Connecting the community to housing resources
Panels, resource fairs, and information sessions connecting Atlanta residents to housing assistance programs, first-time homebuyer resources, rental assistance, and housing stability support. Invite community orgs, housing advocates, and city partners.
Equity Bootcamps
Programming that addresses systemic barriers
Focused sessions on equity-related topics — healthcare access disparities, economic inequity, workforce equity. Educational and actionable. Not advocacy events — resource and information events that help people understand and navigate systems that affect their health.
Events — 3 Core + Brotherhood Brunch
Three events per year — one per pillar. Spread across the year. Aligned to awareness moments. Brotherhood Brunch is an additional element being woven in more intentionally. All events are external and open to the public.
HW-01 · Mental Wellness · Signature
Laughing Matters
Mental Wellness

E100's signature mental health event — using comedy as a vehicle to address mental health challenges. Laughter is proven to reduce stress, lower anxiety, and open people up to difficult conversations. Laughing Matters lowers the barrier of entry to mental health by making it accessible, joyful, and communal.

Format + Purpose
  • Comedy show format — professional comedians + mental health tie-in
  • Panel or resource component alongside entertainment
  • Mental health professionals or licensed counselors available
  • Resources distributed — hotlines, organizations, next steps
  • Public event — open to the Atlanta community
  • Align to June — Men's Mental Health Month
Planning Notes
  • Comedian(s) booked well in advance
  • Mental health partner or clinical org as resource provider
  • Venue: comedy club, theater, or event space — TBD annually
  • Budget: approximately $10,000 · comedians booked by committee
  • Sensitive content guidelines for comedians — mental health is real
  • Photo + recap to Comms (with consent awareness — sensitive topic)
HW-02 · Financial Wellness · Annual
Financial Wellness Event
Financial Wellness

An annual event connecting Atlanta residents to financial wellness resources. Past events have covered private equity, investing, and first-time homebuyer education., education, and expertise. Budgeting, saving, debt management, investing, homeownership — delivered in an accessible, non-judgmental format. Financial health is the foundation of physical and mental health. E100 connects the community to the tools to build it.

Format Options
  • Panel of financial experts and practitioners
  • Workshop format — interactive, hands-on
  • 1-on-1 financial counseling stations
  • Resource fair — banks, credit unions, financial orgs
  • Align to April — Financial Literacy Month
  • Partner with Regions, Bank of America, Equifax, or similar
Planning Notes
  • Corporate financial partners are natural funders here
  • Free to attend — maximize access
  • Include homeownership and credit building specifically
  • Targeted at Black community — address wealth gap directly
  • Photo + recap to Comms within 48hrs
  • Coordinate with Partnerships on financial sector sponsors

HW-04 · Added Element · Growing
Brotherhood Brunch
Added Element

A wellness-adjacent event being woven more intentionally into the H&W calendar. Brotherhood Brunch sits at the intersection of community, connection, and wellness — gathering men for conversation, food, and genuine connection. Currently being integrated more formally as a 4th H&W touchpoint.

Format + Purpose
  • Location changes annually · focused on men's health, check-ups, resources, and wellness
  • Men-focused — community, brotherhood, wellness conversations
  • Speaker or panel component — men sharing openly
  • Mental wellness undertone — without being clinical
  • Bridges the gap between Laughing Matters and a formal mental health event
  • Open to the community — not E100 members only
Planning Notes
  • Being woven in more intentionally each year
  • Timing: coordinate so it doesn't compete with the 3 core events
  • Potential: align to Father's Day or Men's Health Week (June)
  • Low barrier — food, conversation, community. Not intimidating.
  • Photo + recap to Comms within 48hrs

  • Educational and actionable format — not advocacy
  • Community orgs as co-facilitators
  • Align to awareness moments — Black History Month, Juneteenth
