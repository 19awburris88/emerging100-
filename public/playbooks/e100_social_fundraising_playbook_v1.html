The Strategic Purpose
These are not just parties. They are the community-facing fundraising arm of E100. Every event brings Atlanta's professional network into the E100 orbit — some as ticket buyers, some as sponsors, some as future members. The Social Fundraising committee is where E100 shows Atlanta who it is when it's not in a school library or a boardroom.
Relationship to Partnerships: Partnerships raises money year-round and knows most of the sponsors. Social Fundraising coordinates with Partnerships on sponsor outreach — they share the network. A Social Fundraising sponsor may also become a Gala sponsor. The Social deck is the entry point for companies that want brand visibility with a professional audience before committing to a larger program sponsorship.
The Holiday Soirée is the crown jewel. Since 2011. 14th Annual in 2026. 1,500+ attendees. One of Atlanta's premier December events for young professionals. This event has its own legacy, its own brand, and its own audience expectations. It must be planned and executed at the highest level every year.

Relationship to Partnerships & Fundraising
These are sister departments, not competitors. Partnerships raises corporate money year-round. Social Fundraising activates that network at events. They share sponsors, share contacts, and share the goal of funding E100's mission. Coordination is everything.
Social Fundraising Owns
  • ›Event production and execution (all 3 events)
  • ›Venue selection and booking
  • ›Entertainment and talent booking
  • ›Ticket sales and pricing strategy
  • ›Sponsor activation on event nights
  • ›Event marketing and promotion
Partnerships Assists / Coordinates
  • ›Sponsor outreach — they know the contacts
  • ›Closing sponsorship agreements
  • ›Cross-selling Social sponsors into Gala tables
  • ›Post-event stewardship of sponsors
  • ›Tracking sponsors in Monday.com CRM
  • ›Flagging new Social sponsors as Gala prospects
Key coordination point: The Social Fundraising Chair and Partnerships Chair need a regular sync — at minimum monthly in the 6 weeks before each event. Sponsor outreach, closings, and activations must be coordinated. The Social deck is the tool; Partnerships holds the relationships that make it land.
Cross-sell opportunity: Every Social sponsor is a potential Gala table buyer. The 2026 deck explicitly pairs Social packages ($7,500–$30,000) with Gala support ($3,000–$10,000) for a total package of $7,500–$60,000. Partnerships Chair should pitch this combined package to every major Social sponsor.

Marketing Strategy & Asset Production
The theme is everything. Before Eddision Smith can produce a single graphic, the event theme must be locked. Theme → Brief Eddision → Asset production begins. This is the non-negotiable first step in the marketing process for every event.
Campaign starts 6–7 weeks before each event. The Communications committee is a critical partner here — they own the social posting schedule, captions, and distribution. Social Fundraising provides the assets and direction. Comms executes.
Step 1 — Lock the Theme
Before ANY graphic work begins
Theme must be defined, approved, and documented
Every event has its own theme and creative direction — color palette, aesthetic, naming, experience concept. The theme drives everything: the graphics, the venue styling, the sponsor activations, the social content. Lock the theme with the Chair and Exec Board first. Then and only then brief Eddision Smith.
Example: Park 100 2026 Theme
Oak Bluffs / Martha's Vineyard aesthetic. "Welcome to The Bluffs." Culturally rooted, elevated, experiential. Color schemes from the Park 100 PDF. The campaign communicates that guests are not simply attending Park 100 — they are being transported. Theme sets the tone for every asset in the list below.
Step 2 — Brief Eddision Smith
Graphics Designer · All Social Events
Eddision Smith — External Graphic Designer
Creates all social graphics for the three events. When you reach out, you must have: (1) the confirmed theme, (2) the event name, (3) the key asset list with requested delivery dates, and (4) any sponsor/partner logos that need to be included. Track all deliverables and deadlines in Monday.com. When new assets are needed, update the Monday.com tracker and send an email.
⚑ Document Eddision's contact info, rate, turnaround times, and file delivery format in Monday.com so this survives leadership transitions.

Step 4 — Social Media Campaign Schedule
Campaign launches 6–7 weeks before each event. The Communications committee executes the posting schedule. Social Fundraising provides the assets, captions, and direction. Both teams must be in sync — track everything in Monday.com.
7 Weeks Out · Theme Lock
Internal milestone — not public yet. Theme approved. Eddision briefed. Asset tracker opened in Monday.com. Comms team briefed on campaign arc. Save the Date and Theme Reveal assets in production.
6–7 Weeks Out
Save the Date + Theme Reveal: Campaign goes public. Save the Date graphic drops first — announce the timeframe and build anticipation. Theme Reveal follows within days — introduce the event concept and aesthetic. This is the moment that sets the visual identity for the entire campaign.
5–6 Weeks Out
Sponsor Reveals — Individual: Announce sponsors one at a time as they're confirmed. Individual liquor sponsor graphics first, then corporate sponsors. Tag every company. Companies share this content — free reach. Shows the event is backed by serious names.
4–5 Weeks Out
Collective Sponsor Graphic + Talent Reveals: Drop the full sponsor lineup on one graphic. Then reveal DJs, Hosts, and Performers individually. Each talent reveal is its own moment — don't batch them. Build the anticipation piece by piece.
3–4 Weeks Out
Ticket Push + Style Guide Content: Urgency begins. Push tickets actively. Event Style Guide content shows the aesthetic and makes people visualize themselves there. Reel content, behind-the-scenes. Make people feel like they need to be in that room.
2 Weeks Out
Countdown Begins + KBYG: "2 Weeks Away" countdown graphic. Know Before You Go drops — dress code, parking, entry details. This is also when activation teasers drop if applicable.
1 Week → Day Before
Daily Countdown: 1 Week Away → 5 Days Away → 2 Days Away → 1 Day Away. One post per countdown moment. Short, punchy, visual. Ticket urgency. Final push. "See you there" energy.
Event Night
Live Coverage: Stories, reels, live posts. 2 photographers and 2–3 videographers capturing everything. Tag performers, sponsors, and notable attendees. Make people who aren't there feel like they're missing the best night in Atlanta.
48hrs After
Event Recap Post: Best photos and video. Attendance numbers. Scholarship tie-in — what was raised, where it goes. Thank sponsors by name and tag. This post seeds the next event's hype cycle.

Ranges per event. Production costs vary significantly across C&C, Park 100, and Holiday Soirée. Confirm budget with the Treasurer before committing to any vendor or production cost for each event individually.

When does the social media campaign start?
Campaign launch brief goes to Comms 1–2 weeks before the full campaign launches. The campaign itself runs 6–7 weeks leading up to each event. Work backwards from the event date to set the campaign start and brief Comms accordingly.

How does the Gala cross-sell work for Social sponsors?
Social Fundraising is not involved in the Gala cross-sell. That is a Partnerships function only. If a Social sponsor should be pitched on a Gala table, Partnerships handles that conversation — not the SF Chairs.
