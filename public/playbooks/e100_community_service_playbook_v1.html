The Partner On-Ramp ГўВЂВ” From Service to Sponsor
The current reality: organic. Companies serve with E100, relationships are built, and some eventually become Gala sponsors or program partners ГўВЂВ” but there's no formal process for making that conversion happen. This playbook documents the recommended on-ramp so it becomes intentional, not accidental.
Recommended On-Ramp Process
01
Invite to Serve
Community Service chair or member identifies a company that aligns with E100's mission. Extends a personal invitation to serve alongside E100 at an upcoming event. No ask for money ГўВЂВ” just an invitation to do good together. Lower barrier than a sponsorship pitch.
Owner: Community Service Chair
02
Serve Together
Company shows up. E100 members create a warm, professional, impactful experience. Company volunteers feel the mission, see the org in action, and leave with a genuine connection to E100. This is the most important step ГўВЂВ” the experience sells everything else.
Owner: All E100 members present
03
Follow Up Within 48 Hours
Community Service Chair sends a personal thank-you to the company contact within 48 hours. Include photos from the event. Mention the impact made. This is not a sponsorship ask ГўВЂВ” it's relationship stewardship. Keep the warmth alive.
Owner: Community Service Chair
04
Flag & Hand Off to Partnerships
Community Service Chair notifies the Partnerships & Fundraising Chair about the company ГўВЂВ” who the contact is, what event they served at, how it went. This is the formal hand-off. Partnerships now owns the relationship and evaluates fit for Gala sponsorship, program partnership, or deeper engagement.
Owner: Community Service Chair ГўВ†В’ Partnerships Chair
05
Invite Back ГўВЂВ” Deepen the Relationship
Before Partnerships makes a formal ask, invite the company back to another event. Two service experiences > one. By the second time they serve with E100, they feel like part of the family. That's the moment a sponsorship conversation feels natural ГўВЂВ” not transactional.
Owner: Partnerships Chair + Community Service Chair
06
Make the Ask
Partnerships Chair presents the company with a partnership or sponsorship opportunity ГўВЂВ” Gala table, program sponsorship, in-kind support, or annual partnership. By this point the relationship is warm, the company knows what E100 does, and the ask is a natural next step.
Owner: Partnerships Chair
Critical handoff point: Step 4 is where this most often breaks down today. The Community Service Chair must formally flag every new company to the Partnerships Chair after every event ГўВЂВ” not assume someone else will follow up. This is the gap between organic and intentional.
