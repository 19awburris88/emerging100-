GBM Hosting Executive Access Member Development Partner Pipeline v1.0

Corporate Roundtable does two things: it brings E100 into corporate spaces monthly through General Body Meeting hosting, and it creates intimate executive access moments for members. The beauty is in the variety — no two spaces are the same, and every space opens a door.

The Beauty
Variety of Spaces
E100 can go to lots of various spaces. Each new venue is a relationship and a door.
Corporate Roundtable is the physical manifestation of E100's corporate relationships. Every time E100 holds a General Body Meeting inside a Fortune 500 company's office, something happens — members feel the aspiration, the host company sees E100 up close, and the relationship deepens. The room matters. Corporate Roundtable picks the rooms.
Collaboration with Partnerships is essential. Corporate Roundtable opens doors — Partnerships walks through them to fundraise. A company that hosts an E100 General Body Meeting is already warmer than a cold call. Corporate Roundtable and Partnerships must share intel on which companies are engaging and at what level.
General Body Meeting Hosting
Corporate Roundtable owns all GBM venue logistics. The All Chairs Meeting (2nd Monday) and General Body Meeting (3rd Tuesday) are the org's two recurring internal meeting moments. GBMs rotate through corporate partner locations — each one is a member experience and a relationship-building opportunity.

GBM Venue Sourcing Process
01
Identify Host Company
Corporate Roundtable Chair works with Partnerships Chair to identify which corporate partners are appropriate GBM hosts. Hosts should be companies with an existing E100 relationship — either as sponsors, service partners, or active prospects. The GBM is a stewardship and cultivation moment for the host company.
02
Confirm Logistics
Space capacity, parking, AV needs, catering (if any), building access, security requirements. Corporate venues often require guest lists in advance. Coordinate with the host company's point of contact 2–3 weeks before the meeting.
03
Notify Members
Location confirmed to all members via standard communication channel. Include: address, parking info, building entry instructions, dress code if applicable, arrival time. Process & Governance owns the meeting agenda — Corporate Roundtable owns the location logistics.
04
Flag to Partnerships
After every GBM at a corporate location, Corporate Roundtable Chair flags the host company to Partnerships Chair with notes on engagement quality, who the contact was, and whether there's a deeper partnership opportunity. This is the on-ramp from venue host to corporate sponsor.
Executive Access Events
Beyond GBMs, Corporate Roundtable creates intimate executive access moments for members. These are not large events — they are curated, small-group experiences where E100 members get in the room with senior leaders. The goal: professional development, career visibility, and relationship building.
Format
Intimate + Curated
Small groups. Not mass events. The intimacy is the value — it creates real conversation.
Executive Type
C-Suite + Senior Leaders
Fortune 500 executives, community leaders, entrepreneurs. Access members wouldn't normally have.
Member Benefit
Career + Visibility
Members build professional relationships, gain career insight, and increase their own visibility.
Known Examples
Hawks Basketball Game
Jan 2026 — All Chairs at State Farm Arena. Member experience + access. ED owns this variant.
Member prep matters. Before any executive access event, members should be briefed — who they're meeting, what the company does, what's appropriate to discuss. These moments only work if members show up prepared and professional. Corporate Roundtable Chair owns that briefing.
Relationship to Partnerships & Fundraising
Corporate Roundtable and Partnerships are natural partners. Every company that hosts E100 for a GBM is a warm Partnerships lead. Every executive access event deepens a relationship that Partnerships can convert. They must share intel consistently.

✓ Answered
What is the lead time for booking a GBM venue?
Book 2–3 months in advance for our own operational purposes. Some venues may require longer — law firms, federal agencies, or venues with tighter security clearance protocols may need more runway. When in doubt, start earlier.
